Black Pearl Yacht

The 106.7m three-masted sailing yacht, Black Pearl, built by Oceanco and delivered to her owner in 2018, is the result of an evolutionary design by Ken Freivokh Design, Nuvolari Lenard and Villate De¬sign. A multi-level central atrium includes an all- glass lift that rises alongside the central mast, contributing to a feeling of vertical integration between the decks. Dykstra Naval Architects designed Black Pearl's DynaRig carbon masts and sailing system.
All 2,900 square metres of sails can be set at the push of a button in 7 minutes! BMT Nigel Gee collaborated with Oceanco to create Black Pearl's hybrid propulsion system as well as other onboard systems such as waste heat recovery. In addition to wind power, she is powered by 2 x 1080kW MTU's / 2 x 400kW electrical propulsion motors. Her accommodations include a master suite, 2 VIP suites, 2 double guest cabins and a full beam beach club, that is convert¬ible into a cinema.

Athena Yacht

Built in 2004 by Royal Huisman, Athena was the largest sailing yacht in the world at 90 metres when delivered by the Dutch yard. The superyacht's exterior lines were designed by Pieter Beeldsnijder Design, with naval architecture by Dykstra Naval Architects and an interior by Rebecca Bradley Interior Design. She can sleep up to 10 guests across five staterooms, with a crew of up to 18. Today Athena measures as the fourth largest sailing yacht in the world after EOS, Black Pearl and Sailing Yacht A.

Maltese Falcon Yacht

The Maltese Falcon is a vessel that can truly be said to have revolutionized the world of superyachts. Launched in Italy in 2006 by Perini Navi, she has retained her revolutionary status ever since. Her three massive rotating masts each supports up to five sails and power the yacht to a speed in excess of 24 knots, while she cruises at 16 knots. At 88 meters, she is one of the fastest sailing superyachts in the world: a fully breathtaking beast in performance.
In addition, Maltese Falcon also comes with a huge array of toys and tenders, including two 10 meter ribs, a sailing dinghy, stand up paddleboards, two jet skis and plenty of towable toys. As a result of all of these amenities and her stunning interiors, she has become one of the most popular large sailing yachts for charter, almost rivaling motor yachts.

Aquijo Yacht

At 85m in length, the Tripp-designed Aquijo is the largest high performance ketch in the world. Built by a first time partnership between Oceanco and Vitters, she is not merely motoryacht with sails. Her twin 90-metre masts carry identical 1,192-square-meter mainsails. With all up, including code sail, her total sail area leaps to a staggering 5,051 square metres. Because of the enormity of the project, all items such as shrouds, winches, jib sheets had to be custom made.
The clean modern interior, designed by Dolker + Voges, was designed to be flexible with multi-purpose spaces. Large windows provide light and a connection to the outdoor spaces. The project began in 2010 and was delivered in 2016. This is a first class sailing vessel designed to cruise around the world.

Sea Eagle Yacht

Sea Eagle is a sailing yacht with a length of 81.0m. The yacht's builder is Royal Huisman from Netherlands who delivered superyacht Sea Eagle in 2020. The superyacht has a beam of 12.0m, a draft of 6.0m and a volume of 1,104 GT.
The Sea Eagle, previously named Sea Eagle II, features exterior design by Dykstra Naval Architects, while her interior was penned by Mark Whiteley Design, with naval architecture by Dykstra Naval Architects. Up to 12 guests are accommodated on board the superyacht Sea Eagle, and she also has accommodation for 13 crew members including the captain of Sea Eagle. The yacht Sea Eagle has a aluminium hull and aluminium superstructure. She is powered by 2 Caterpillar Inc engines, which give her a top speed of 21.0 kn.
